About Me

Hiya, I'm Maya Toole!

I'm a Graphic Designer based in Pensacola, Florida, with a love for expressive and playful design. I've been absorbed in art of all kinds for as long as I can remember, and I began learning digital design when I was just 13. Since then, and especially now, I've been drawn to bold visuals and work that doesn't shy away from personality.

I believe good design should be immediately recognizable regardless of variation. I aim to create simple and effective designs that can stand out on their own or amongst a crowd, through bold linework, unexpected splashes of color, or just whimsical designing.

When I'm not making, you'll likely find me poring over various moth species, or building fantasy worlds for my friends' next D&D adventure.

Right now, I'm looking for job opportunities where I can grow, collaborate, and bring a creative spark to the team. If you think I might be a good fit, let's chat!

Relevant Work

Graphic Designer & Production Assistant
– Firefly Reproductions

August 2023 – Current

At Firefly Reproductions, I created and adapted custom designs using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and Lightroom. I worked closely with clients to bring their ideas to life, preparing artwork for sublimation, heat transfer, and UV flatbed printing. Beyond designing, I operated and maintained production equipment, helping manage tight deadlines while keeping quality a priority. During my time there, I expanded my skills into new printing methods and got hands-on experience with the full digital-to-physical production process.

Graphic Designer & Screen Print Assistant
– Pace Monogramming Inc.

October 2021 – August 2023

At Pace Monogramming, I split my time between designing custom artwork and working hands-on with screen printing. Using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and CorelDraw, I created and adapted designs based on client specifications, working with a wide range of customers — from individuals and businesses to schools, universities, and churches. Workload balance was essential to success every day, as anywhere between 50 - 100 orders passed through my hands a week. Outside of design, I helped with the full screen printing process: prepping, cleaning, and inventorying screens, as well as printing on garments. During my time there, I worked on projects for clients like the University of West Florida, the University of South Alabama, and the University of Florida A&M, with some designs even reaching customers as far as Ohio.
